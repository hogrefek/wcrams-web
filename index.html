Welcome to the WC-RAMS!

The Wallowa County Rangeland Assessment and Monitoring System (WC-RAMS) unites community knowledge with computer modeling technolgy in a system that provides ranchers, other resource managers, and local stakeholders in the land (wherever they currently live) with the ability to identify and manage risks to critical rangeland resources.

Key Features

Short-term Risk Assessment
The PHYGROW/LEWS model provides 0-90 day plant biomass forecast maps allowing for livestock management (herd location, composition or size) in response to local weather patterns and/or drought.
Long-term Risk Assessment
The LandTrendr model provides maps of habitat change over the past 30 years, allowing for linkage of habitat response to management practices and enabling discussions about whether particular changes pose risks that should be avoided or opportunities to be pursued.
Power of "Big Data"
CBON methods and current technolgy allow the WC-RAMS to leverage the power of troves of satellite imagery, climate prodcts, and rangeland condition data that are free access from goernmental and other sources, while also contibutin to efforts to improve it.
Protection of Local Data and Interests
CBON protocols protect community privacy rights through ownership agreemnensts and empower them with documentation of local scientific knowledge in a natter that will hold up to any scinetific or legal scrutiny.
